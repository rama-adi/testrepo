# Facts About Seals

## Physical Characteristics
- Seals are marine mammals with streamlined bodies designed for swimming
- They have thick blubber layers (up to 6 inches) that provide insulation in cold water
- Their flippers evolved from limbs and allow them to move both in water and on land
- Most seals have large eyes adapted for low-light underwater vision

## Behavior & Habitat
- Seals are found primarily in cold ocean waters, with species in both Arctic and Antarctic regions
- They are carnivorous and primarily feed on fish and squid
- Seals are excellent divers and can hold their breath for extended periods (up to 20+ minutes)
- They are social animals and often rest in large groups called "haul-outs"

## Communication & Intelligence
- Seals communicate underwater using clicks, whistles, and barks
- They have excellent hearing both underwater and in air
- Seals demonstrate problem-solving abilities and can be trained to perform tasks

## Breeding & Lifecycle
- Most seal species breed once a year during specific seasons
- Mothers nurse their pups with nutrient-rich milk that allows rapid growth
- Pups typically spend several weeks with their mothers before becoming independent
- Seals can live 20-40 years depending on the species

## Species Diversity
- There are approximately 33 species of seals worldwide
- Common species include harbor seals, gray seals, harp seals, and leopard seals
- Some species, like the monk seal, are critically endangered
